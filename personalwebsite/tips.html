Tips to Improve

Nail the Basics


Good skiing starts with strong fundamentals. Keep your knees bent, weight centered, and practice balancing to stay in control. Building a solid foundation on easier trails helps you advance more quickly and confidently.

Quick Tip: Focus on a low, balanced stance with hands forward to avoid leaning back.

Turn Like a Pro


Efficient turns make all the difference, especially on steeper slopes. Practice smooth, controlled S-turns by shifting your weight from one ski edge to the other. With time, turning will feel like second nature.

Quick Tip: Use a gentle pole plant before each turn to keep rhythm and stay balanced.

Build Confidence Gradually


Take on steeper slopes step-by-step. Start with trails that are a bit challenging but manageable, focusing on controlled turns and steady form. Progressing slowly helps build skill and reduces the chance of injury.

Quick Tip: Visualize your line down the slope and take each section one turn at a time.
